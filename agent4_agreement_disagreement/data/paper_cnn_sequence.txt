Convolutional Sequence to Sequence Learning: CNN as a Viable Alternative

We find that convolutional neural networks can match or exceed transformer performance on multiple sequence modeling tasks.
Our experiments demonstrate that attention mechanisms are not strictly necessary for achieving high-quality NLP results.
The proposed CNN architecture achieves 26.1 BLEU on WMT English-German, which is competitive with attention-based approaches.
We show that locality in convolutions naturally captures relevant n-gram patterns more efficiently than global attention.
Unlike transformers, our model scales linearly with sequence length rather than quadratically.
Results indicate that architectural simplicity does not inherently sacrifice translation accuracy.
We demonstrate that pre-training is beneficial but requires proper inductive bias to be effective.
Our findings suggest that the NLP community may over-rely on attention-based models when simpler architectures suffice.
The computational cost of our approach is significantly lower than transformer models at inference time.
We conclude that for many practical NLP tasks, convolutional models offer a favorable accuracy-efficiency tradeoff.
