Attention Is All You Need: A Transformer Architecture for Sequence Modeling

We propose the Transformer, a model architecture relying entirely on an attention mechanism, dispensing with recurrence and convolutions entirely.
Our model achieves 28.4 BLEU on the WMT 2014 English-to-German translation task, outperforming the best previously reported models including ensembles by more than 2 BLEU.
We demonstrate that the Transformer can be trained significantly faster than architectures based on recurrent or convolutional layers.
On the WMT 2014 English-to-French translation task, our model establishes a new single-model state-of-the-art BLEU score of 41.0.
Results show that multi-head attention enables the model to jointly attend to information from different representation subspaces at different positions.
We show that self-attention layers are more interpretable than recurrent layers in terms of positional attention patterns.
Pre-training on large corpora dramatically improves downstream task performance across all evaluated benchmarks.
Our experiments confirm that model depth and number of attention heads are critical hyperparameters for performance.
The Transformer architecture requires significantly less computation to train than recurrent architectures given comparable model capacity.
We find that positional encodings are sufficient to capture sequential information without explicit recurrence.
