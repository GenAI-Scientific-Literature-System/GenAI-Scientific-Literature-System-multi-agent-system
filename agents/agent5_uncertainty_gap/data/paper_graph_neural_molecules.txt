Graph Neural Networks for Molecular Property Prediction

We present a novel graph neural network architecture for molecular property prediction in drug discovery.
The model perhaps captures long-range atomic interactions more effectively than existing message-passing approaches.
Evaluation on larger molecular databases is needed to confirm scalability beyond our current benchmark.
We do not address the problem of out-of-distribution molecules from novel chemical scaffolds.
The computational cost scales poorly for molecular graphs with more than 1000 heavy atoms.
We suggest that incorporating edge attention over bond types may further improve performance, but this remains untested.
A significant limitation of our approach is the reliance on hand-crafted molecular fingerprints as initial features.
Future directions include end-to-end learning of molecular representations without domain-specific feature engineering.
It remains unknown whether this approach transfers effectively to protein-ligand interaction prediction tasks.
Open challenges include real-time inference for virtual screening and reliable uncertainty quantification.
We could not obtain sufficient labeled data for rare disease targets, limiting evaluation scope.
Further experiments with 3D molecular conformations are needed but were beyond the scope of this study.
