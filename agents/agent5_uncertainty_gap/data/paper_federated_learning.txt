Federated Learning: Open Problems and Research Directions

This survey highlights several open and unsolved problems in federated learning for privacy-preserving AI.
Data heterogeneity across participating clients remains a fundamental unsolved challenge with no universally accepted solution.
Privacy guarantees in practical federated settings are not yet well understood beyond differential privacy bounds.
We identify a critical benchmark gap: no standard federated NLP benchmark dataset currently exists for fair comparison.
Communication efficiency in large-scale cross-device settings is an underexplored area with significant practical implications.
The lack of interpretability and explainability tools for federated models is a significant limitation for regulated industries.
Future research should urgently address fairness and representation across heterogeneous client populations.
We could not find sufficient peer-reviewed studies on adversarial robustness in federated learning under Byzantine attacks.
Broader implications for healthcare, finance, and legal deployment of federated systems are currently unclear.
Further investigation of Byzantine-robust aggregation methods is warranted before large-scale real-world deployment.
It is unclear whether federated learning can match centralized learning on complex generative tasks.
We assume that clients behave honestly during training, which may not hold in adversarial settings.
