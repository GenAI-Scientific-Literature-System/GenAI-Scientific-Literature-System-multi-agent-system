Uncertainty in Clinical NLP: A Transformer-Based Approach

We suggest that transformer models may improve performance in clinical NLP tasks under certain conditions.
However, our study is limited by a small sample size of n=14 patients drawn from a single hospital.
Future work should explore larger and more diverse patient populations to validate these findings.
The generalizability of these results to other medical specialties and non-English clinical text remains unclear.
We assume that the annotated dataset is representative of real-world clinical notes, but this has not been independently validated.
Further investigation is needed to confirm these preliminary results before clinical deployment.
Our model perhaps captures clinical entity relationships more accurately than rule-based systems, though this requires replication.
The lack of a standardized benchmark for clinical NLP makes cross-study comparison difficult.
We do not address the problem of model behavior on rare diseases due to insufficient training data.
Open questions remain about model calibration, fairness across patient demographics, and long-term reliability in production environments.
The broader impact of deploying such models in clinical decision support is unknown and warrants careful evaluation.
We leave adversarial robustness evaluation for future research, as it is beyond the scope of this preliminary study.
